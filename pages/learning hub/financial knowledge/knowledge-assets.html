What are assets and liabilites?

Assets vs. Liabilities

Assets are resources that provide future benefits, while liabilities are obligations that must be repaid. Understanding the difference helps young adults see the positive and negative sides of financial health.

  • Assets: savings, property, investments, or skills that generate value
  • Liabilities: loans, credit card debt, or unpaid obligations
  • Assets build stability, liabilities reduce it if unmanaged

Why the Difference Matters

Assets represent ownership and control, showing the strength of someone’s financial situation. Liabilities, on the other hand, highlight debts and responsibilities
